Arnab Rai Choudhuri

Professor

Ph.D.: University of Chicago (1985)
Visiting positions:
High Altitude Observatory, Boulder (1985-87)
University of Chicago (1989))
University of St. Andrews (1991)
Kiepenheuer Institut, Freiburg (as Humboldt Fellow, 1994-95)
Montana State University (2000)
Max-Planck- Institut, Lindau (2002)
Eotvos University, Budapest (2004)
Cambridge University (2004)
Beijing Astronomical Observatory (2006)
National Astronomical Observatory of Japan (2012)
Research Interests:
Plasma astrophysics. The origin of solar magnetic cycle. Formation of sunspots. Magnetic fields of neutron stars and pulsars. History of science.

He is the author of two advanced textbooks The Physics of Fluids and Plasmas: An Introduction for Astrophysicists (Cambridge University Press, 1998) and Astrophysics for Physicists (Cambridge University Press, 2010) as well as a popular science book Nature’s Third Cycle: A Story of Sunspots (Oxford University Press, 2015). He is a Fellow of the three science academies of India and The World Academy of Sciences (TWAS). He was an Alexander von Humboldt Fellow and a JC Bose National Fellow.
